PC Building

Build your dream machine from scratch!

Why Build Your Own PC?

Building your own computer is one of the most rewarding projects in all of nerd culture. Instead of buying a pre-built machine, you choose every single part yourself — like assembling your own custom superhero. The result is a computer that is perfectly tailored to what you need, whether that is gaming at ultra-high settings, video editing, streaming, or everyday use.

Beyond the performance benefits, building a PC is a fantastic learning experience. You will gain a deep understanding of how computers work, and you will have the satisfaction of pressing that power button for the first time knowing that you put the whole thing together. Many people describe building their first PC as a life milestone — and it is much less intimidating than it sounds!

The Core Components

Every PC is made up of several key parts. Here is what each one does:

  • CPU (Central Processing Unit) – The "brain" of the computer. Handles all the calculations and runs your programs. Popular brands: AMD Ryzen and Intel Core.
  • GPU (Graphics Processing Unit) – Processes visuals and renders graphics. Critical for gaming and video work. Popular brands: NVIDIA GeForce and AMD Radeon.
  • RAM (Random Access Memory) – Short-term memory for your PC. More RAM means more programs can run at once without slowdowns. 16GB is a solid starting point.
  • Motherboard – The main circuit board that everything connects to. Must be compatible with your CPU.
  • Storage (SSD or HDD) – Where your files, games, and operating system are stored. SSDs are much faster than older HDDs.
  • PSU (Power Supply Unit) – Converts wall power to the voltages your components need. A reliable PSU is very important for safety.
  • PC Case – Houses and protects all your components. Comes in many styles from budget to showpiece builds with tempered glass panels.
  • CPU Cooler – Keeps your CPU from overheating. Options include air coolers (fans) and liquid coolers (AIO).

Building Your PC Step by Step

The build process is like a careful puzzle. Here is an overview of the main steps:

1

Plan Your Build

Decide on your budget and what you will use the PC for. Use PCPartPicker to pick compatible parts and compare prices. Watch YouTube build guides for your specific parts before you start.

2

Gather Your Tools

You only need a Phillips-head screwdriver (magnetic tip is very helpful), a well-lit workspace, and an anti-static wristband or the habit of touching your metal case before handling components.

3

Install CPU and RAM on Motherboard

Before putting the motherboard in the case, install the CPU (gently — no force needed!), apply thermal paste, attach the CPU cooler, and click in your RAM sticks.

4

Mount Motherboard and Storage

Screw the motherboard into the case using the included standoffs, then install your SSD or M.2 drive according to your motherboard manual.

5

Install GPU and PSU

Seat the graphics card into the PCIe slot and connect it to the power supply. Route your PSU cables neatly to keep airflow good inside the case.

6

First Boot and OS Install

Connect monitor, keyboard, and mouse, then press power! If everything lights up, install Windows or Linux from a USB drive and enjoy your new machine.
